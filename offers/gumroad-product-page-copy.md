Product Summary

Stackfax checks whether your tools, models, subscriptions, hardware, workflow, agents, automations, permissions, and cost risks match the task.

You send your current stack, use case, workflow, and concerns.

Stackfax sends back a manual report with a rating, Stack Score, risk flags, verdict, and best next move.

The goal is simple:

Clarity before you build, buy, automate, or deploy.

How It Works

1. Buy the Stackfax Quick Report.
2. Complete the intake form.
3. Stackfax reviews your stack manually.
4. Stackfax asks follow-up questions if needed.
5. Stackfax prepares your report.
6. Your report is delivered by email or another approved delivery method.
7. Optional feedback may help improve future reports.

⸻

Delivery Window

Expected delivery target:

24 to 48 hours after intake is received and accepted.

Delivery may take longer if:

* your intake is incomplete
* your stack is unclear
* follow-up questions are needed
* the request is outside the current Stackfax scope
* unsafe private information needs to be removed
* the workflow involves business systems, customer data, credentials, payments, wallets, or production systems

If delivery is delayed, Stackfax should send a clear update when possible.

⸻

Intake Requirement

The report cannot begin until the intake is submitted.

The intake should describe:

* your goal
* your current stack
* your tools and subscriptions
* your hardware plans
* your local, cloud, or hybrid preference
* your workflow
* your cost concerns
* your risk concerns
* your automation comfort level
* your approval gates
* the decision you want the report to help with

A good intake should answer:

* What are you trying to build or improve?
* What are you using now?
* What feels confusing, expensive, risky, or overbuilt?
* What should the system be allowed to do?
* What should require human approval?
* What decision should the report help you make?

⸻

Important Safety Rule

Do not submit:

* passwords
* API keys
* recovery codes
* payment details
* wallet information
* seed phrases
* private keys
* private customer data
* private legal documents
* private medical documents
* private financial account information
* sensitive personal information
* identity documents
* employee records
* client confidential files
* private inbox exports
* source code secrets
* production credentials

Stackfax only needs a description of your stack, workflow, tools, goals, risks, and concerns.

Core principle:

Stackfax reviews the stack problem, not your private life.

⸻

If Sensitive Information Is Submitted

If you submit sensitive information, Stackfax may pause the report and ask for a cleaned-up intake.

Stackfax should not use sensitive information, copy it into a report, or store it in the public repo.

The report can continue only after the intake is safe.

Early Manual Status

Stackfax Quick Reports are early and manual.

This means:

* a human reviews the intake
* a human prepares the report
* report structure may improve over time
* feedback may improve future templates
* reports may vary as the format is refined
* reports are guidance, not guarantees

Do not pretend the report is fully automated.

Manual review is part of the early product.

⸻

Refund Expectation

If Stackfax cannot deliver your report, you should receive a refund.

If your intake is missing key details, Stackfax may ask follow-up questions before starting.

If your intake contains sensitive or unsafe information, Stackfax may pause the report until a cleaned-up intake is provided.

Because reports are manual and custom, delivered reports are generally reviewed case by case for refund requests.

Refund handling should stay simple, fair, and trust-first.
