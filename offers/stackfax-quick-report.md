Stackfax Quick Report

The Stackfax Quick Report is an early manual report for people who want clarity before they buy hardware, burn premium-model tokens, copy an Ai agent setup, automate a workflow, or build the wrong stack.

Stackfax checks whether the user’s tools, models, subscriptions, workflows, agents, and hardware match the job before they spend more money, burn more tokens, or overbuild.

⸻

Status

This offer is early and manual.

The goal is to test the Stackfax report format, learn from real stacks, and help users make better Ai stack decisions before the full product is built.

Stackfax should not pretend this report is fully automated before it is.

Manual review is part of the early product.

⸻

Who It Is For

The Stackfax Quick Report is for:

* beginners who do not know what stack they need
* early builders trying to avoid expensive mistakes
* builders who want to avoid bottlenecks later
* OpenClaw users comparing setup options
* people considering a Mac mini or local Ai box
* people burning too many premium-model tokens
* people unsure which model subscriptions they actually need
* local Ai users unsure whether their hardware matches their workflow
* agent builders unsure what should require human approval
* small businesses testing automation before it touches real operations
* operators who want a second opinion before buying, building, automating, or deploying

Avoid public language like “noob.”

Use beginner, early builder, first-time builder, operator, team, or small business owner.

Example Intake Questions

Users may be asked:

1. What are you trying to build or improve?
2. What decision do you need help making?
3. What tools, models, subscriptions, agents, or automations are you using?
4. Are you considering hardware like a Mac mini, local Ai box, GPU, or server?
5. Are you cloud-first, local-first, hybrid, or unsure?
6. What workflow are you trying to run?
7. What are you most worried about: cost, privacy, token burn, setup, scaling, safety, or reliability?
8. What actions would the stack be allowed to take?
9. What should require human approval?
10. What currently feels confusing, expensive, risky, or overbuilt?
11. Do you have any public links or non-sensitive screenshots that help explain the setup?

Do not ask users to send passwords, API keys, payment details, wallet information, private customer records, or sensitive documents.

⸻

Core Rule

Cheap model drafts.

Strong model decides.

Human approves anything touching customers, money, inventory, credentials, private files, public posting, or production systems.

The model can request an action.

The system decides whether that action is allowed.

⸻

Report Logic

A good Stackfax Quick Report should answer:

* Can this setup work?
* Is it safe enough for the intended workflow?
* What will it likely cost?
* Where might it waste tokens or time?
* What should be fixed first?
* What should stay manual?
* What should require approval?
* What proof should exist if something breaks?

The user should finish with a clear next move.
