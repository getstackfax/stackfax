# Stackfax Consultant Wedge

Stackfax can help Ai consultants, automation agencies, and bot sellers turn messy buyer interest into a clearer first pilot conversation.

## Core Idea

You sell the automation.

Stackfax makes the buyer feel safer saying yes.

## The Problem

Many Ai automation sellers run into the same issue:

- too many vague leads
- too many messy business requests
- too many custom builds
- too much unpaid discovery
- too many buyers asking for the biggest setup first
- too many risky workflows with customer data, credentials, money, or production systems
- too little time to scope every prospect properly

A buyer may be interested, but not ready for a giant quote.

A seller may be capable, but overloaded.

Stackfax creates a useful pause between interest and proposal.

## The Stackfax Role

Stackfax acts as a pre-build stack check.

The buyer submits their current tools, workflow, goals, and concerns.

Stackfax returns a report on:

- what fits
- what looks overbuilt
- what should stay human-approved
- where the risk is
- whether a smaller pilot makes more sense
- what should be rechecked before scaling

## Why Consultants Use It

Stackfax gives consultants:

- cleaner intake
- warmer leads
- better-scoped prospects
- a reason to follow up
- a trust artifact before the proposal
- a way to recommend a smaller pilot without sounding weak
- a way to avoid reckless automation promises

## Buyer-Friendly Pitch

Before I sell you a big Ai setup, let’s get your current stack checked.

Stackfax gives you a simple report on what fits, what looks overbuilt, what should stay human-approved, and what first pilot makes sense.

Then we can talk about the build with less guessing.

## Consultant-Friendly Pitch

Stop doing free discovery calls for messy Ai ideas.

Send the stack through Stackfax first.

Use the report to turn vague automation interest into a cleaner pilot conversation.

## Core Positioning

Stackfax does not compete with the consultant.

Stackfax makes the consultant look safer.

## Sales Flow

1. Consultant finds an interested business owner.
2. Consultant says the setup should be checked before quoting a large build.
3. Buyer completes Stackfax intake.
4. Stackfax returns a report.
5. Consultant uses the report to recommend the first pilot.
6. Buyer feels safer.
7. Consultant closes with better scope.

## Best Early Partner

The best early Stackfax consultant partner is not a giant agency.

It is a mid-tier operator who is already selling Ai bots, agents, automations, or workflows to small businesses and needs a better way to qualify prospects.

Good signs:

- posting real builds
- talking to small businesses
- selling automations
- overloaded with leads
- doing too much unpaid discovery
- willing to start with smaller pilots
- cares about buyer trust
