# Stackfax Beta Launch Soft Post

This file contains soft launch post drafts for early Stackfax beta users.

The goal is to invite a small number of real users without spamming, overpromising, or pretending Stackfax is more finished than it is.

## Core Goal

Find early users who are already confused about Ai stacks, OpenClaw setup, model subscriptions, local hardware, token burn, or business automation safety.

## Core Message

Stackfax is the first report card for Ai stacks.

It helps people check whether their tools, models, subscriptions, hardware, workflow, and permissions actually fit the task.

Clarity before you build.

## Soft Launch Post 1

I’m building Stackfax — the first report card for Ai stacks.

The idea is simple:

Before you buy hardware, burn premium-model tokens, pay for overlapping subscriptions, or copy the wrong OpenClaw setup, Stackfax checks whether your stack actually fits the job.

Early reports are manual.

I’m looking for a few beta stacks to review.

Best fit if you’re asking:

- Do I need a Mac mini?
- Which Ai subscription should I pick?
- Am I about to burn tokens?
- Is my OpenClaw setup overkill?
- What should agents be allowed to touch?
- Should I use local models, cloud models, or both?

Stackfax sends back a simple report with a rating, risk flags, verdict, and best next move.
