Stackfax Beta Launch Soft Post

This file contains soft launch post drafts for early Stackfax beta users.

The goal is to invite a small number of real users without spamming, overpromising, or pretending Stackfax is more finished than it is.

⸻

Core Goal

Find early users who are already confused about Ai stacks, OpenClaw setup, model subscriptions, local hardware, token burn, workflow fit, agent permissions, or business automation safety.

The goal is not to create hype.

The goal is to learn from real stack questions and test whether the report format helps.

⸻

Core Message

Stackfax is the report card for Ai stacks.

It helps people check whether their tools, models, subscriptions, hardware, workflows, agents, permissions, approval gates, and cost risks actually fit the task.

Clarity before you build, buy, automate, or deploy.

⸻

Soft Launch Post 1

I’m building Stackfax — the report card for Ai stacks.

The idea is simple:

Before you buy hardware, burn premium-model tokens, pay for overlapping subscriptions, automate the wrong workflow, or copy the wrong OpenClaw setup, Stackfax checks whether your stack actually fits the job.

Early reports are manual.

I’m looking for a few beta stacks to review so I can improve the report format.

Best fit if you’re asking:

* Do I need local hardware?
* Which Ai subscription should I pick?
* Am I about to burn tokens?
* Is my OpenClaw setup overkill?
* What should agents be allowed to touch?
* Should I use local models, cloud models, or both?
* What should require human approval?
* What proof should exist if something breaks?

Stackfax sends back a simple report with a rating, risk flags, verdict, and best next move.

Trust Rules Before Posting

Before posting publicly, confirm:

* intake form exists
* payment or free beta path is clear
* delivery method is clear
* contact email is ready
* private folder is ready
* expectations are clear
* no fake testimonials are used
* no guaranteed outcomes are promised
* Stackfax is described as early/manual
* users are not asked to post sensitive data publicly
* refund and delivery terms are clear if money is involved

Do Not Do

Do not:

* spam unrelated threads
* drop links without context
* pretend to be a neutral user
* overclaim savings
* promise guaranteed outcomes
* say Stackfax is automated before it is
* push paid reports where a free helpful reply is better
* collect sensitive private information publicly
* imply security, legal, financial, medical, trading, or business guarantees
* DM people cold just because they mentioned an Ai stack problem
* turn every useful reply into a Stackfax pitch
* make the beta feel bigger than it is
