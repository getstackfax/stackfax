# Stackfax

The report card for Ai stacks.

Before you buy the Mac mini, burn premium-model tokens, or copy a random Reddit OpenClaw setup, Stackfax your stack.

Go to Stackfax.com and get your free stack report.

## What is Stackfax?

Stackfax helps people understand, score, and improve their Ai stacks.

An Ai stack is the combination of models, providers, tools, agents, memory, hosting, permissions, workflows, and approval gates used to get work done with Ai.

Most people do not need one giant expensive model for everything. They need the right stack for the job.

## What does a Stackfax Report show?

A Stackfax Report checks your stack for:

- Cost risk
- Token burn
- Hardware overkill
- Noob safety
- Privacy risk
- Missing tools
- Model routing
- Approval gates
- Use-case fit
- Upgrade path

## Core Rule

Cheap model drafts.  
Strong model decides.  
Human approves anything touching customers, money, inventory, credentials, or production systems.

## Why Stackfax exists

Ai stacks are getting confusing, expensive, and risky.

Beginners do not know what they need.  
Builders do not want to waste tokens.  
Businesses need approval gates before automation touches real operations.  
Creators and experts want to compare, flex, and improve their stacks.

Stackfax gives people a simple report before they spend money, copy a stack, or automate something important.
