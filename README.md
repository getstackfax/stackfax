# Stackfax

The first report card for Ai stacks.

Clarity before you build.

Before you buy hardware, burn premium-model tokens, pay for overlapping subscriptions, or copy a random OpenClaw setup, Stackfax your stack.

Go to getstackfax.com and get your free stack report.

Stackfax checks whether your tools, models, subscriptions, and hardware match the task before you spend money, burn tokens, or overbuild.

## What is Stackfax?

Stackfax helps people understand, score, and improve their Ai stacks.

An Ai stack is the combination of models, providers, tools, subscriptions, agents, memory, hosting, hardware, permissions, workflows, and approval gates used to get work done with Ai.

Most people do not need one giant expensive model, one perfect machine, or every subscription at once. They need the right stack for the job.

## What does Stackfax solve?

Stackfax helps users answer questions like:

- What stack do I actually need?
- Do I need a Mac mini, cloud server, or better stack design?
- Am I paying for overlapping model subscriptions?
- Am I using expensive models for simple work?
- Is this OpenClaw setup overkill for my use case?
- What should run locally, in the cloud, or manually?
- Where could tokens drain?
- What should require human approval?
- What might break when this stack scales?

The first Stackfax wedge is the hardware verdict.
